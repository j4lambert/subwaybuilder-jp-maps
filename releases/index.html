subwaybuilder-jp-maps

A collection of maps for the game SubwayBuilder featuring the Japanese Archipelago.

These map packs are constructed from Japanese public demographic, employment, and infrastructure datasets. The pipeline normalizes multiple data sources into a shared municipal/neighborhood geography, generates simulation points, reconciles those points to official totals, and produces commute-oriented origin/destination demand suitable for gameplay.

Data Construction (High Level)

  1. Administrative boundary and neighborhood geometry are collected and clipped to each target city region.
  2. Resident and worker signals are ingested from official census/economic statistics at both neighborhood and mesh-grid levels.
  3. Candidate demand points are seeded from those signals to represent local population and job concentration.
  4. Point-level totals are balanced so neighborhood and grid-level aggregates remain consistent with source statistics.
  5. Origin/destination flows are assigned using distance-aware gravity-style allocation calibrated to municipality-level commute structure.
  6. Optional facility-driven demand (for example airports, ports, hospitals, and schools) is injected as additional demand where supported by source data.
Sources
  • e-Stat (Statistics Bureau of Japan): https://www.e-stat.go.jp/
  • e-Stat GIS (small-area boundaries and spatial datasets): https://www.e-stat.go.jp/gis
  • 国土数値情報 / National Land Numerical Information (MLIT): https://nlftp.mlit.go.jp/ksj/
  • GTFS-GIS (derived 100m population mesh): https://gtfs-gis.jp/teikyo/
  • OpenStreetMap (context features such as roads/buildings/water): https://www.openstreetmap.org/
